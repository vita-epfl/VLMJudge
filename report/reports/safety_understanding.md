# Safety understanding — eight-model audit

On the 2,164-pair Safety category, the six open-source baselines cluster between 30.3% and 42.7%, with Qwen2.5-Omni leading and InternVL3.5-30B trailing; the agentic Qwen3-VL pipeline (v4o) jumps to 58.3% and the hinted variant (v4p) regresses to 50.2%. Errors are overwhelmingly **optimistic** in every model. The binary `Yes/No` task is essentially a "Yes" classifier for all baselines (between 50.6% and 57.9% accuracy, only marginally above the 52.6% Yes-prior); the 1–3 Likert task is the actual divider. Confusion tables make the asymmetry stark: Cosmos-Reason maps 489 of 581 GT=3 clips and 445 of 485 GT=1 clips to the midpoint "2"; InternVL3.5-30B maps 974 of its 994 Likert mistakes to "2"; Qwen3-VL alone splits its mass between "2" (584) and "3" (498), refusing to ever emit "1". The agent breaks this — v4o emits "1" 121 times (vs. 0 for base Qwen3-VL on those same 103 clips it gets right, all of which the base model called "2" or "3") — and the hints undo it (v4p: 16 ones).

**Cross-model failure modes.** *Absence-of-evidence-as-safety* dominates every baseline: the model only audits ego actuation (smoothness, lane position, following distance) and concludes safe whenever no abrupt maneuver is visible, ignoring whether the ego ran a stop sign or red light. *(InternVL3.5-8B, aggressive_takeover/agg_tk_001_Rainy.mp4)*: "The driving appears steady and cautious, with no abrupt maneuvers or dangerous driving observed... Overall, the driving seems mostly safe with minor issues." The same heuristic survives the agent: *(v4p, crossing_red_lights/cross_red_006_Snowy.mp4)*: "consistent pink/magenta motion... no signs of erratic braking or steering... safe driving behavior." *Weather-as-only-risk fallacy* — fog/rain/snow on `negative` clips is read as the sole determinant, flipping safe ground truth to unsafe; for InternVL3.5-30B 99 of its 102 Yes→No errors are weather-tagged. *(InternVL3.5-30B, negative/agg_tk_038_Foggy.mp4)*: "the reduced visibility due to fog can be hazardous... without knowing if these precautions are taken, [it is] not entirely safe." *Likert mode-collapse on "2"* — Cosmos-Reason, InternVL3.5-30B, InternVL3.5-8B and LLaVA-OneVision emit "2" for ≥ 81% of all Likert items (Cosmos: 970/1082; InternVL-30B: 1011/1082); since 67% of the Likert ground truth is the extremes (485 GT=1, 597 GT=3), the constant-2 strategy floors them at ≈ 9-13% accuracy. *Stop-sign rolling-stop blindness* — across baselines, clips in `crossing_stop/` where the ego rolls through without halting are read as "approaches and stops appropriately"; binary accuracy on this folder is 14-25% across all baselines. *(Qwen3-VL, crossing_stop/cross_stop_033_Original.mp4)*: "approaches a stop sign without any apparent issues... maintains a steady and controlled speed... following traffic rules by stopping at the stop sign" — for a clip whose ground truth is the violation.

**Model-specific quirks.** Qwen3-VL has the highest baseline binary accuracy (53.1%) but the lowest *overall* benchmark accuracy because its safety binary is the only category where it commits decisively to the "Yes" prior; on Reality understanding it collapses to 0.7%, and the slack lives there, not in safety. Qwen2.5-Omni's 28.9% Likert (best non-agent) comes from a near-uniform 555/520 split between "3" and "2" rather than from any genuine danger detection — it still emits "1" only 7 times out of 1082, almost all incorrect. Cosmos-Reason is the only baseline that occasionally emits a free-form essay in place of a parseable token (e.g. *"The safety of the ego car depends on its ability to navigate the glare and cones cautiously..."* on `negative/...Golden hour.mp4`), reflecting its instruction-tuned reasoning bias; the answer-extraction regex catches enough of these that its parse failures stay at single digits, but the 31 GT=3→"3" rate (5.3%) is the worst correctly-emitted rate of any model. LLaVA-OneVision combines mode-collapse on "2" (874/1082) with the lowest binary accuracy on `crossing_red_lights` (12.5%), driven by its inability to register the red-light cue at all — it parses traffic-light state correctly on only 18/144 isolated red-light prompts elsewhere in the benchmark.

**Why the agent route helps.** Base Qwen3-VL Likert is 26.7%, v4o is 50.9%; the entire delta is concentrated in `crossing_red_lights` (Likert 0.7% → 58.6%) and `negative` (47.6% → 78%), with `crossing_stop` (8.4% → 8.4%) and `aggressive_takeover` (0% → 0.6%) effectively unchanged. The agent's optical-flow tool is mentioned in 99% of v4o failure raw_outputs, but the *useful* signal is a SOP-C priority rule that promotes the flow result above the language-prior smoothness checklist. On the 103 dangerous clips v4o newly catches, the rationale is consistently of the form *(v4o, crossing_red_lights/cross_red_037_Foggy.mp4)*: "The optical flow video shows a strong pink/magenta pattern, indicating forward motion... the car is moving forward through the intersection [while] the traffic lights are red. This constitutes a traffic violation... dangerous." The same prompt without the agent (base Qwen3-VL) on those exact 103 clips returns "2" 61 times and "3" 42 times — never "1". The optical-flow tool does not measure danger directly; it provides a single binary "ego is moving" cue that, combined with red-light visibility from the original frames, breaks the "no abrupt maneuver = safe" trap.

**Why hints hurt.** v4p adds hint sub-questions ("Describe the scene...", "Are there cones/cyclists/...") that must be answered before the safety verdict. This regresses v4o's 58.6% red-light Likert to **5.9%** and 78.3% red-light binary to **7.2%**, while marginally improving `negative` accuracy (78% → 82.8% Likert; 92.4% → 97.2% binary). The mechanism is visible in the raw outputs: hint answers absorb the agent's attention budget and the optical-flow priority rule is no longer the conclusion the model commits to. *(v4p, crossing_red_lights/cross_red_038_Morning.mp4)*: "stopping appropriately at the red light. There are no signs of sudden braking, erratic steering, or unsafe maneuvers. The car stops before the crosswalk... The driving is compliant with traffic rules" — for a ground-truth red-light violation. The Likert prediction histogram makes the regression mechanical: v4o emits "1" 121 times, v4p only 16; v4p reverts to the pre-agent failure mode where dynamic violation cues are overruled by static smoothness checks. The hints are a textbook example of cue dilution — adding subroutine grounding work re-enables the language-prior heuristic that the SOP-C rule was specifically designed to suppress.
