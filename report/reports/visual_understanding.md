## Visual understanding — baseline failure analysis (304 items)

The Visual-understanding category (159 highway, 144 traffic-light, 1 lighting) is near-saturated for seven of the eight evaluated models: InternVL3.5-30B 95.1%, Cosmos-Reason and Qwen2.5-Omni 94.1%, InternVL3.5-8B and Qwen3-VL 90.5%, Qwen3-VL+agent variants 90.1 / 91.4%. LLaVA-OneVision is the single outlier at 52.3%, almost entirely driven by a complete collapse on "Are the traffic lights red ?" (12.5%, 18/144). The agent route (v4o) does not improve visual understanding — it actually trails the strongest baseline by 5 points and exposes a new tool-induced failure mode.

**LLaVA: a constant "the lights are green" hallucination on red-light clips.** Of LLaVA's 145 visual-understanding failures, 126 are on the red-light question, and 125/126 are GT=Yes answered "No" — a one-sided collapse, not random noise. Inspection of ~30 raw_outputs reveals a near-template response that confidently asserts the lights are *green* even on clips whose folder name (`crossing_red_lights/cross_red_*`) and visible signal indicate red. Across `cross_red_036_Original`, `_001_Foggy`, `_039_Morning`, `_042_Snowy`, `_036_Rainy`, `_036_Snowy` LLaVA emits the same one-liner: "The traffic lights are not red; they are green" (LLaVA, `cross_red_036_Rainy`). On harder lighting (foggy / golden hour) it manufactures green even when admitting low visibility: "the traffic lights are visible in some of the images, and they are green" (LLaVA, `cross_red_037_Foggy`). Two clips trigger an even more diagnostic mistake — the model invents a *blue* traffic light: "they are blue… signaling for vehicles to proceed" (LLaVA, `cross_red_009_Snowy`). One clip (`cross_red_008_Sunny`) sends LLaVA into an unbounded "image N shows red, image N+1 shows green" loop that hits the parse-fail bucket. The behaviour is consistent with a model that fails to ground the colour token on the actual red-bulb pixels and falls back on a "moving car ⇒ green light" prior — every justification cites motion through the intersection rather than the signal itself.

**Cross-model residuals (5–10%) cluster in three failure modes.** All seven other models share the same pattern, just at low rate.

- *Highway over-recognition.* 18/18 highway misses for LLaVA, all 5 highway misses for InternVL3.5-30B, 4/5 for Qwen3-VL etc. are urban-arterial / divided-suburban clips (`aggressive_takeover/agg_tk_022/023/032/034`) confidently classified as highway from a fixed cue list (multi-lane, guardrails, semi-trucks, overhead signs). Qwen3-VL on `agg_tk_032_Snowy` (GT=No): "guardrails, the expansive layout… confirm that the ego car is on a highway".
- *Red-bulb-anywhere → "Yes".* On the 6 GT=No red-light clips, every baseline produces false positives: InternVL3.5-30B, Qwen2.5-Omni and Cosmos-Reason all flip Yes on `cross_red_025_Rainy`, `_025_Snowy`, `_038_Night`, `_010_Golden hour`, `_018_Golden hour`, `_009_Rainy`. The models do not separate the ego-lane signal from cross-traffic / left-turn / glare-on-wet-road signals. Qwen2.5-Omni on `cross_red_010_Golden hour` (GT=No): "the traffic lights are clearly visible in the video and they are showing red".
- *Temporal-quantifier inversion (Qwen3-VL specific).* Qwen3-VL frequently sees the red signal correctly, then answers "No" because the light *changes* during the clip: "The traffic lights are red at the beginning of the video, but they change to green… therefore, the traffic lights are not red" (Qwen3-VL, `cross_red_036_Original`). 9 of Qwen3-VL's 23 red-light errors follow this template — a literal-quantifier reading rather than a perception failure.

**The agent route does not help and introduces format-level failures.** Qwen3-VL+agent (90.1%) is below plain Qwen3-VL on the highway question (94.3 vs 96.9) and below Cosmos-Reason / InternVL3.5-30B / Qwen2.5-Omni overall. 18 of 30 agent failures are `NO MATCH !` — the agent never emits `final_answer` and gets cut off mid-rationalisation. The cause is over-routing trivial colour-cue questions to the optical-flow tool, then arguing with its output: on `cross_red_036_Foggy` the agent loops "the optical flow shows a strong pink/magenta pattern, which indicates forward motion of the ego vehicle. This forward motion is occurring while the vehicle is stopped at an intersection" (Qwen3-VL+agent) without ever closing the answer. Even when it converges, the tool detour produces contradictory chains: "consistent pink and magenta patterns, indicating forward motion… however, the vehicle is stopped at an intersection, and the traffic lights are visibly red" (Qwen3-VL+agent, `cross_red_038_Night`). Adding hand-written hints (v4p, +1.3 pp) recovers most of the routing error but still trails the no-tool baselines — confirming that for static, single-frame perceptual cues the agent adds latency and parse risk without a perception gain.
