# Artifacts understanding — cross-model audit (8 systems)

The category contains 727 (video, question) pairs from cosmos-predict1, split between *Do some objects change shape or appearance?* (n=402) and *Does any object appear or disappear without continuous motion?* (n=325). Ground truth is overwhelmingly Yes (~88%, 643/727), so a model that always answers No can sit near 12% accuracy without ever seeing an artefact. Six of the eight base models cluster in exactly that band: Qwen2.5-Omni 13.1%, InternVL3.5-30B 13.6%, Qwen3-VL 15.3%, Cosmos-Reason 17.5%, InternVL3.5-8B 20.1% — Qwen3-VL is in fact a *strict* constant-No classifier (0/616 failures contain "Yes"), and InternVL3.5-30B is barely better (1 single Yes among 628 failures). LLaVA-OneVision is the only base model whose Yes-rate is meaningfully above zero (123/727, 16.9%) and is therefore mechanically the highest at 25.2%, but its precision on Yes is 90.2%, indistinguishable from the GT prior — i.e. it answers Yes randomly often enough to harvest some true positives, not because it sees the artefacts. Shape-change is uniformly worse than appear/disappear (e.g. Cosmos-Reason 10.9 / 25.5; InternVL3.5-30B 5.7 / 23.4) because the silhouette-stability heuristic (below) maps directly onto the shape-change question. The agentic route flips the regime: Qwen3-VL+agent (v4o) climbs to 58.5%, with the Yes-rate now 59.0% — the only system that is actually discriminating. Adding hint scaffolding (v4p) drops the category to 46.8%, and it is appear/disappear that collapses (52.0 → 28.3) while shape-change is essentially preserved (63.7 → 61.7).

**Cross-model failure modes.**

- *Continuous-motion over-attribution.* The phrase "continuous motion" appears in 33–41% of every base-model failure file (Qwen2.5-Omni 257/632, LLaVA 231/544, Cosmos-Reason 239/600). The model treats steady forward camera flow as a license to deny *any* discontinuity. *"all objects, such as cars, trees, and road signs, are visible throughout the sequence without sudden appearances or disappearances"* (Qwen3-VL, `cross_stop_003_Night.mp4`); *"the video shows a continuous scene of a highway with cars and the environment moving smoothly"* (InternVL3.5-30B, `scB_bYG27yI_101952_7b.mp4`).
- *Stable-silhouette ⇒ no artefact.* Identity-preservation is conflated with rigidity. Phrases like "maintain their shape" or "do not change" appear in 233 Qwen3-VL failures and 280 Qwen2.5-Omni failures (≈40–45%). *"the elements remain static and do not undergo any transformations"* (InternVL3.5-30B, `Qb5UT3pSxjI_29328_14b.mp4`); *"a person walking … changes position but maintains the same shape"* (Qwen3-VL, `pedestrians_022_Golden hour.mp4`).
- *Weather / lighting laundering.* Fog, rain, snow and night cover ~25–40% of failures and are consistently invoked to neutralise blur and morphing. *"snowfall and lighting conditions might cause some changes in appearance, but there are no significant changes"* (InternVL3.5-8B, `cross_stop_003_Night.mp4`); *"the fog reduces visibility, making the scene appear more uniform … no objects change shape"* (LLaVA-OneVision, `car_crash_015_Foggy.mp4`). This mirrors the "dashcam-aesthetic laundering" GPT-5.4-mini exhibits on the reality binary.
- *Plausibility-over-evidence.* Cosmos-Reason recasts the question as a sanity check on the *world*, not the *frames*: *"the objects (cars, traffic lights, road signs, etc.) are described as moving consistently"* — note "described as", the model is reasoning over its own caption, not the pixels — *"objects … maintain their original shape"* (Cosmos-Reason, `pedestrians_016_Snowy.mp4`). LLaVA's single FP comment shows the same reflex used the other way: *"the person's shape and appearance remain consistent, but their position changes"* (LLaVA, `pedestrians_004_Golden hour.mp4`) — Yes was the correct call but for the wrong reason.

**Model-specific quirks.**

- LLaVA-OneVision (25.2%) is *not* doing artefact detection. Its overall (Yes+No) confusion is TP 111 / FP 12 / TN 72 / FN 532; precision-on-Yes is 90.2% and recall-on-Yes is 17.3% — within sampling noise of a weighted-coin baseline given 88% positive priors. Its Yes-calls are dominated by mis-mapping legitimate scene events (pedestrians walking out, traffic-light state-change) onto the question: *"Both individuals are stationary and do not move … objects appear or disappear without continuous motion"* (`car_crash_004_Morning.mp4`, FP). The 25% headline is essentially a base-rate artefact, not a real lift over Qwen3-VL.
- Cosmos-Reason (17.5%) is a near-constant-No classifier *with* generous internal monologue. Yes-rate 8.4% (61/727), of which 9 are FPs; the remainder (52) are correct Yeses but driven by hallucinated artefacts ("a small rectangular object … disappears abruptly", `scB_bYG27yI_54480_7b.mp4`, GT=No counted as FP). It denies artefacts more often than it claims them, contradicting the headline narrative that a "reason" model would be better here.
- InternVL3.5-30B (13.6%) underperforms its 8B sibling (20.1%). The mechanism is visible in the confusion matrices: 30B emits Yes only 17 times out of 727 (2.3%) versus 8B's 76 (10.5%). 30B is *more* confidently No-defaulting than the smaller model — likely because RLHF-style alignment on the larger checkpoint sharpens the "be cautious / don't fabricate" prior, which on this benchmark is the wrong default. 30B never localises to a frame index; its outputs are uniformly generic ("the elements remain consistent", 196/628 failures use "maintain shape"-class phrasing vs 72/581 for 8B).
- Qwen3-VL is mechanically incapable of saying Yes here: 0 Yes answers across all 616 failures. Its baseline on appear/disappear (22.2%) and shape-change (9.7%) is *exactly* the GT=No prior — confirming the system has zero artefact signal in its native video pipeline.

**Why does the agent route help?** v4o's correct answers are dominated by two tool citations Qwen3-VL alone never produces. (i) FFT anisotropy with the 0.048 threshold appears in 382 of 425 correct calls. *"FFT analysis returned an anisotropy score of 0.0588, which exceeds the 0.048 threshold for real footage"* (`car_crash_007_Snowy.mp4`); *"anisotropy score of 0.0508, which is above the 0.048 threshold … SAM segmentation revealed significant rigidity violations"* (`uvRaCzWpFrQ_69264_7b.mp4`). (ii) SAM-mask "rigidity violation / texture swimming / component morphing" vocabulary appears in 378 of 425. *"the roof of the car deforms and melts, the body wobbles unnaturally, the wheels warp and merge into the body"* (`jKrJezTsVUk_65520_14b.mp4`). FFT supplies a per-clip prior on AI-genness; SAM forces the VLM to look at object crops in isolation, breaking the global "scene looks coherent" frame that drives every base model's stable-silhouette excuse. The combination is what closes the gap from 15.3 to 58.5.

**Why do hints hurt?** v4p collapses appear/disappear to 28.3% (vs 52.0 for v4o) while leaving shape-change essentially intact. The hint set for that question is exactly two strict cues — *"Do vehicles 'pop' into existence in the middle of the road? Do distant cars flicker in and out of visibility?"* (`data_preparation/resources/hint_questions.json`). The model uses them as a closed checklist: if no vehicle pops and nothing flickers, answer No, regardless of FFT/SAM evidence. 139 of 165 v4o-Yes calls flip to No under v4p with this exact pattern. *"FFT analysis indicates an anisotropy score of 0.0570 … above the 0.048 threshold … However, the SAM segmentation … shows no evidence of objects appearing or disappearing without continuous motion … the artifacts observed … do not manifest as objects appearing or disappearing without motion"* (v4p, `Qb5UT3pSxjI_51120_14b.mp4`); *"FFT analysis indicates the video may be AI-generated, but the visual inspection using object masks shows no evidence"* (v4p, `MXxot9M4-iE_58800_14b.mp4`). The hint suppresses the prior (FFT) by privileging a literal reading of "pop / flicker", which the cosmos-predict1 generators rarely produce — they morph and breathe instead. Shape-change hints (*"car models morph", "text on signs scramble"*) align with what FFT/SAM already flag, so v4p stays close to v4o on that question.
