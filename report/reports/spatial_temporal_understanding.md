The Spatial-temporal category collapses to two questions: 133 overtake-side items (GT=Right in 127/133) and 201 binary "Has the ego car stopped?" items (GT=No in 197/201). On the binary, six of eight models are saturated (InternVL3.5-30B 79.6%, InternVL3.5-8B 90.0%, Qwen3-VL/Omni 93.5%, agent v4o 96.0%, agent+hints v4p 97.5%); LLaVA-OneVision sits at 8.5% and Cosmos-Reason at 52.7%. On overtake-side, every model except the dataset's own "Right" prior collapses: Qwen2.5-Omni 2.3%, Qwen3-VL 3.8%, agent+hints 6.8%, InternVL3.5-8B 7.5%, agent v4o 12.0%, LLaVA 17.3%, InternVL3.5-30B 18.8%, Cosmos-Reason 25.6%. Even the agent route — Qwen3-VL armed with optical-flow (RAFT), SAM, and FFT — recovers only 16/133. The motion is perceived; the side label is wrong. This is a labelling-convention failure, not a perception failure.

Three failure modes recur across all eight models.

**Reference-frame inversion** dominates wherever a model commits to a side. The chain-of-thought correctly localises a slower vehicle on the ego's right but maps the maneuver to the lane the ego *enters*, not the lane it leaves the slower car in. Cosmos-Reason on `aggressive_takeover/agg_tk_001_Rainy`: "the ego car overtakes those vehicles and moves into the adjacent lane to their left", answer Left, GT Right. Qwen3-VL on `agg_tk_031_Morning`: "overtakes the truck from the left side", answer Left. Agent v4o on `agg_tk_001_Rainy`: "pink/magenta flow pattern on the left side... overtaking the white car from the left", answer Left. Agent+hints on `agg_tk_024_Morning`: "ego car moving into the left lane to pass a semi-truck that is in the right lane", answer Left — geometry stated correctly, label inverted. The benchmark labels the side of the *overtaken* vehicle relative to the ego; the models label the side of the *ego* relative to the overtaken vehicle. The two conventions are opposites, so every coherent perception flips.

**Lane-of-ego confusion** is the same bug at sentence level. InternVL3.5-30B on `agg_tk_015_Night`: "moving from the right lane to the left lane to overtake them", answer Left, GT Right. Qwen3-VL on `agg_tk_017_Snowy` even chains both directions in one sentence: "moving past the truck from the left lane to the right lane, indicating that it is overtaking on the left." The model writes the correct lane transition and still produces the inverted label.

**Refusal / "not overtaking" hedge** absorbs cases where the small Qwen-family models cannot resolve five sparse frames into a maneuver: Qwen2.5-Omni 116/130, InternVL3.5-8B 102/123, Qwen3-VL 106/128 of overtake failures. Qwen3-VL on `agg_tk_001_Rainy`: "the camera's perspective shows the car moving forward... maintaining its lane without passing any other vehicles." InternVL3.5-8B on `agg_tk_033_Sunny`: "the car is simply driving in the right lane, following the flow of traffic" — same scene, same hedge. The models prefer abstaining over committing to a side.

Two model-specific quirks deserve mention.

**LLaVA-OneVision** answers Yes on 182/185 of its stopped-failures and No on zero — a near-constant-Yes mode anchored to the visible STOP sign rather than to inter-frame ego motion. On `cross_stop_055_Night`: "the ego car has stopped at the intersection, as indicated by the 'STOP' sign visible in the video." On `cross_stop_010_Golden hour`: "stopped at the intersection, as indicated by the 'STOP' sign visible in the frame." The static landmark is read as evidence of stopping; landmark-displacement reasoning, which works for every other model, is absent. This collapses LLaVA's Spatial-temporal accuracy to 13.0%.

**Cosmos-Reason** is paradoxically the strongest on overtake-side (25.6%) and the weakest on stopped (52.7%). The mechanism is that it is the only baseline that almost never hedges: of its 99 overtake failures, 86 are confident Right→Left flips and only 12 are "not overtaking", versus 102/123 hedges for InternVL3.5-8B. Committing to a side delivers 29/115 GT=Right correct hits by chance; the others hedge them away. The same commit-bias hurts on stopped: Cosmos produces 95 false-positive stops out of 96 stop failures — it answers Yes whenever it sees a stop sign or stop-line in the frame, mirroring the LLaVA mode in miniature. High commitment is an asset on the 3-way overtake question and a liability on the binary.

**Why doesn't the agent help?** Qwen3-VL+agent v4o has FFT, SAM, and RAFT optical flow, and it visibly *uses* RAFT — the raw outputs cite "pink/magenta flow patterns" on most overtake items. Yet agent v4o scores 12.0%, agent+hints v4p only 6.8%, and v4p commits Left on 118/124 failures. The convention error is downstream of perception. RAFT correctly returns lateral motion; the prompt resolves it as "overtaking on the left side" because the model maps "side where the ego is" instead of "side where the overtaken car is." Adding hints amplifies, not corrects, this — v4p is more confident, so it flips harder. On `agg_tk_002_Original` v4o writes "the ego car's forward motion relative to the other vehicles, confirming the overtaking maneuver is occurring on the left" — the tool succeeded, the labelling step failed.

**Implications.** A right-skewed dataset disguises this as a vision deficit; in fact every model that inspects the geometry describes it correctly. A single prompt sentence — "report the side of the *overtaken* vehicle, not the side of the ego" — would, by the structure of the confusion matrices, recover ~70% of the missed Rights for Cosmos-Reason and the agent variants and convert most "not overtaking" hedges in the Qwen baselines into committed Rights. The agent's tool stack is unnecessary for this question; the failure lives entirely in the labelling convention. The Gemini audit reached the same conclusion on a different model; eight more models confirm it.
