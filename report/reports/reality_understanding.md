# Reality understanding

## Headline

Across the 3 172 (video, question) pairs of the *Reality understanding* slice — all drawn from `cosmos-drive-dreams` and all therefore "Generated" / Likert ∈ {1, 2} — the eight models cluster into three regimes. Five non-agent baselines sit between **15% and 32%** with one shared confusion direction: a near-uniform "Real" verdict on the binary (1 291–1 597 of 1 597 binary items, i.e. 80–100% false-negative rate) and an upward Likert drift of one to two bins. Qwen3-VL collapses to the absolute "Real" prior (binary acc. **0.7%**, 1 586/1 597 false negatives) and is the worst model on this slice. InternVL3.5-30B (31.6%) is the strongest non-agent purely because it mode-collapses the Likert onto "2", which happens to be the GT for 887/1 574 score items. The Qwen3-VL+agent route lifts binary accuracy to **74.6%** and overall reality to **57.5%** by routing the question to a frequency-domain tool; adding hints (v4p) drops the model to **42.8%** because the hint prompt re-introduces a visual-reasoning veto that overrides the FFT cue.

## Cross-model failure modes

- **"Real" prior driven by absence-of-artifact reasoning.** All six baselines (Cosmos-Reason, both InternVLs, both Qwens, LLaVA) explicitly enumerate what is *not* present and conclude Real. *"There are no obvious signs of digital artifacts or inconsistencies that would suggest it is generated by AI"* (Qwen3-VL, `negative/agg_tk_030_Foggy.mp4`); *"Generated videos often have subtle but noticeable artifacts… this video does not display such signs"* (InternVL3.5-30B, `crossing_red_lights/cross_red_036_Original.mp4`). The world-model output has been engineered precisely to remove the cues these models look for, so absence is the norm.
- **Dashcam-aesthetic laundering.** Lens flare, motion blur, fisheye distortion and windshield reflections are read as proof of physical capture. *"The fisheye lens effect is typical of dashcams… realistic environmental details such as palm trees, streetlights"* (Qwen3-VL, `crossing_red_lights/cross_red_022_Night.mp4`); *"slight blurriness and artifacts suggest it might be captured from a moving vehicle"* (InternVL3.5-30B, `aggressive_takeover/agg_tk_019_Sunny.mp4`); *"the consistent presence of a dashboard at the bottom of the frame… typical of a dashcam recording"* (LLaVA-OneVision, `negative/agg_tk_030_Foggy.mp4`). The same cosmos-drive-dreams hood overlay is treated as authentication evidence by every baseline.
- **Weather-as-cover.** `*_Foggy`, `*_Snowy`, `*_Rainy`, `*_Night` and `*_Golden hour` clips dominate the binary failures: reduced visibility supplies a ready-made excuse for the blur and softness that would otherwise be flagged. *"The natural distortion caused by fog, and the subtle motion blur all suggest authentic environmental conditions"* (Qwen3-VL, `negative/cross_stop_034_Foggy.mp4`); *"natural lighting transitions, consistent motion blur"* (Qwen3-VL, `negative/0c8e48f7…_Golden hour.mp4`).
- **Likert one-bin upward drift.** Across Cosmos-Reason, InternVL3.5-8B, Qwen3-VL and Qwen2.5-Omni, the dominant Likert error is GT=2 → 3 (522–867 cases per model) with a near-symmetric GT=1 → 2/3 spillover. Quoted reasoning admits "minor issues" then awards 3: *"makes it difficult to distinguish from a real-life recording. Answer: 3"* (Qwen3-VL, GT=2, `negative/e255e893…_Original.mp4`). LLaVA-OneVision is the exception — it parks every score on "2".
- **Cross-question inconsistency.** Models routinely flag suspicious cues in the Likert reasoning then declare Real on the paired binary for the same clip. Cosmos-Reason on `negative/7f3bbe35…_Morning.mp4` notes *"the white car… brake lights on… doesn't make sense in a real scenario… could indicate something artificial"* and answers Real anyway. The constraint that every video is "Generated" + Likert ∈ {1, 2} is invisible to all six baselines.

## Model-specific quirks

- **Qwen3-VL — collapsed "Real" prior (0.7%).** The model produces the same templated paragraph almost verbatim across thousands of clips (*"natural motion blur, consistent lighting… no visible artifacts or inconsistencies"*) regardless of source variant. Its Likert is similarly degenerate: 867/1 574 GT=2 are pushed to 3 and 548/1 574 GT=1 are pushed to 3. The model has effectively learned "dashcam input ⇒ Real, score=3" as a closed-loop response.
- **LLaVA-OneVision — exact 0.000 binary accuracy.** All 1 597/1 597 binary items get "Real". On the Likert it parks on "2" (959/1 574 outputs), which mechanically yields 40.6% by collision with the GT distribution. There is no internal cue selection — every paragraph reduces to "consistent dashboard / lighting / vehicles ⇒ Real".
- **InternVL3.5-30B — strongest non-agent (31.6%).** The lift comes entirely from the Likert: 887 GT=2 → 2 hits (vs Qwen3-VL's 96) plus 531 GT=1 → 2 (a one-step error rather than a two-step one). The model never emits "1", so it cannot get GT=1 right but pays only one bin per error rather than two. Its binary is still 7.3%, with the same dashcam-laundering quotes as the rest.
- **Cosmos-Reason — best non-agent on the binary (19.0%).** Unlike its peers, its chain-of-thought sometimes catches inconsistencies (*"the unnatural behavior… could indicate something artificial"*, `negative/7f3bbe35…_Morning.mp4`; *"cars seemingly passing through each other"*, `crossing_stop/cross_stop_036_Snowy.mp4`). It still defaults to Real on 1 291/1 597 cases, but the 303 hits show a working — if low-recall — generative-artifact taxonomy that the other open models lack.

## Why the agent route helps (v4o, 57.5%)

The base Qwen3-VL has no working cue for reality detection; the agent rewrites the task as a tool call. The decisive tool is **FFT anisotropy** (`fft/`): with a fixed 0.048 threshold, frequency-domain energy distribution flags world-model output regardless of its visual gloss. Across the 1 597 binary items, 1 189 produced anisotropy > 0.048 and v4o answered "Generated" on 1 180 of those (**99.2%** routing fidelity). SAM segmentation acts as a second pass for object-level rigidity / morphing checks; RAFT and SSIM are the optical-flow / temporal-consistency back-ups invoked when FFT is borderline. Successful calls quote the score directly: *"FFT anisotropy 0.0519 exceeds the 0.048 threshold… directional energy patterns typical of synthetic videos"* (`negative/f059738a…_Snowy.mp4`).

Three residual failure modes survive the agent. (i) **Sub-threshold synthetics** — 402 items have FFT < 0.048 and the agent (correctly given the metric) answers Real on 396; these are the high-fidelity Original / Sunny / Snowy variants where the world model's frequency signature falls inside the natural range. (ii) **Visual override on the Likert** — even with FFT flagging generation, the agent grades the scene 3 when SAM finds no morph: *"The FFT analysis returned an anisotropy score of 0.0464, below 0.048… the scene is indistinguishable from real footage"* (GT=2, `negative/a823962c…_Morning.mp4`). The Likert path doesn't always pull the FFT verdict through. (iii) **Self-contradiction artefacts of the prompt format** — repeated `evaluation:` / `answer:` blocks where the agent "thinks aloud" twice and the parser keeps the wrong one, e.g. `negative/ebe1f268…_Morning.mp4` (GT=2, parsed as 3).

## Why +hints is worse than v4o (42.8% vs 57.5%)

The hint prompt explicitly invites the model to weigh visual evidence against the FFT score, and that veto path is exactly what costs accuracy. On binary items where FFT > 0.048, v4o answers "Generated" on **99.2%** (1 180/1 189); v4p drops to **84.6%** (893/1 055) and 140 v4p binaries no longer carry an FFT score at all (vs 6 for v4o), suggesting the hint sometimes routes around the tool. The override is verbatim: *"The FFT analysis indicates a high anisotropy score (0.0496), suggesting potential AI generation. However, the SAM segmentation masks show no signs of rigidity violations… the visual evidence of physical consistency outweighs the statistical anomaly"* (v4p, `negative/cross_stop_014_Original.mp4`); *"FFT result is a strong indicator of generation, but the visual evidence is clean… the video is likely real"* (v4p, `negative/agg_tk_039_Snowy.mp4`). Because cosmos-drive-dreams clips are visually clean by construction, "no SAM artifacts" is the modal observation, so any hint that promotes visual reasoning to a tie-breaker preferentially flips correct FFT verdicts to Real. The same effect drags the Likert: v4p emits "3" on 521 GT=2 items (vs 235 for v4o) and `final_answer not called` traces show duplicate "evaluation:/answer:" blocks where the second pass overwrites the first with a higher score. Hints, in short, re-import the same dashcam-aesthetic-laundering fallacy that the bare agent had successfully bypassed.
