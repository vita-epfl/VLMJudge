# Traffic laws understanding — eight-model audit

On the 665-pair Traffic-laws category, the six open-source baselines collapse to between 1.5% (LLaVA-OneVision) and 23.0% (Cosmos-Reason); the agentic Qwen3-VL pipeline jumps to 65.0% (v4o) and the hinted variant climbs further to **73.1% (v4p) — the only category in the entire benchmark where v4p beats v4o**. The accuracy floor is set by the dominant `Is the ego car following the traffic rules?` question (n=512, 510 of which are GT=No), where confusion tables expose pure constant-Yes behavior: LLaVA-OneVision answers "Yes" on 655 of 665 items (No-class recall 0/655), InternVL3.5-8B 652/665, Qwen2.5-Omni 618/655 GT=No, InternVL3.5-30B 617/655, Qwen3-VL 600/655, Cosmos-Reason 503/645. Every baseline correctly recovers the 10 GT=Yes items by default; none flag more than 9% of the 655 violations. The two near-saturated questions ("Are the traffic lights red?", "Has the ego car stopped?") sit at 88-94% for all non-LLaVA baselines because they are answerable from a single salient frame; the failure mass concentrates in `aggressive_takeover/`, `crossing_red_lights/` and `crossing_stop/` — folders whose names announce that the ego is violating, but whose violations are encoded in trajectory rather than a single static cue.

**Cross-model failure modes.** *Constant-Yes default with templated rationale.* On GT=No clips, baselines reach for a fixed "safe distance / within lane / following traffic flow" checklist regardless of the violation subset; Qwen2.5-Omni emits "safe distance" in 31% of GT=No outputs, "flow of traffic" in 23%, and Qwen3-VL the same at 28% / 25%. *(Qwen2.5-Omni, aggressive_takeover/agg_tk_017_Night.mp4)*: "driving in the correct lane, maintaining a safe distance... no visible signs of reckless driving." *Sub-frame red-light interpolation* — when frame N shows the car stopped at red and frame N+1 shows it past the line, the model invents an off-screen green transition: this phrase pattern occurs in 33% of Cosmos-Reason GT=No outputs, 26% Qwen2.5-Omni, 21% Qwen3-VL. *(Cosmos-Reason, crossing_red_lights/cross_red_040_Rainy.mp4)*: "stops behind other vehicles... when the lights turn green, the car proceeds through the intersection." The light is red the entire clip. *Stop-sign attribution to compliant cross-street* — the model concedes a stop sign is visible, then claims it governs the intersecting road and excuses the ego from stopping. *(InternVL3.5-30B, crossing_stop/cross_stop_041_Original.mp4)*: "obeying the speed limit (as inferred from the smooth flow of traffic)... no instances of running... stop signs, or other violations." *Narrow violation vocabulary* — "no visible violations" / "no indications of" appears in 36% of InternVL3.5-30B and 34% of Qwen2.5-Omni GT=No outputs; the checklist is limited to "speeding, lane change, red light, stop sign", so unsafe overtaking, tailgating, and shoulder driving never trigger a No verdict. *(LLaVA-OneVision, aggressive_takeover/agg_tk_032_Snowy.mp4)*: "blue lights on the vehicles ahead indicate that they are following the rules as well." *Weather-as-cautious-driving excuse* (Cosmos-Reason 26%, others <5%): rain/fog/snow are explicitly cited as evidence of compliance because "the driver is taking precautions". *(Cosmos-Reason, aggressive_takeover/agg_tk_006_Rainy.mp4)*: "using windshield wipers, so they're taking necessary precautions for the conditions. That's a point in their favor."

**Model-specific quirks.** *InternVL3.5-8B (1.8% on follow-rules) and LLaVA-OneVision (1.6%)* are textbook constant-Yes classifiers — InternVL-8B emits No on only 3 of 665 traffic items, LLaVA on 0; both refuse to instantiate the violation class on this folder despite handling "Has the ego car stopped?" at 90% / 8%. *Cosmos-Reason* is the strongest non-agent at 23% by emitting No 145 times — five to fifty times more often than its peers — and is the only baseline whose `crossing_stop` follow-rules accuracy (37.8%) substantially exceeds the others (Qwen3-VL 8%, InternVL-30B 6%, Qwen2.5-Omni 8%); its instruction-tuned reasoning chain occasionally arrives at the right verdict by enumerating the stop-sign rule in plain text *(Cosmos-Reason, crossing_red_lights/cross_red_022_Night.mp4)*: "the ego car turns left while the light is red. That's a problem because running a red light is a violation." It still loses `aggressive_takeover` (9.4%) for the same trajectory-blindness reason as the others. *LLaVA-OneVision* uniquely tanks on "Are the traffic lights red?" at 12.5% (138 GT=Yes, 131 LLaVA-No) because it misreads red phases as green: *(LLaVA-OneVision, crossing_red_lights/cross_red_022_Night.mp4)*: "various traffic lights in different states, including green and yellow, but never red" — the entire clip frames a red light. This single perception failure cascades into a 1.3% accuracy on the light-cue question. *Qwen3-VL* shows the highest light-cue accuracy among baselines (23.5%) but the lowest "Has the ego car stopped?" parsing-clean rate among the non-LLaVA models, suggesting its single-frame light reasoning is intact while its multi-frame motion reasoning is the weakest of the cohort.

**Why the agent helps so much.** Base Qwen3-VL on follow-rules is 5.7%; v4o is 59.4%. The entire +54-pt jump is in `crossing_red_lights` (5.7% → **94.7%**) and `crossing_stop` (5.7% → **75.6%**); `aggressive_takeover` stays at 5.6%. The mechanism is the optical-flow tool plus an SOP-C priority rule that the agent must commit to a violation verdict whenever forward motion (pink/magenta flow) is observed *while* the static cue (red light, stop line, stop sign) is in frame. The agent reasons explicitly about both signals being simultaneously present. *(v4o, crossing_red_lights/cross_red_022_Original.mp4)*: "strong pink/magenta motion, indicating the ego car is moving forward... while the traffic lights are red... violating traffic rules by proceeding through a red light." This grounds the verdict in *measured* motion rather than in the language-model prior that "stopped vehicle in frame 1 + advanced vehicle in frame 4 = legal stop happened in between". The agent inherits the baseline's vocabulary failure on `aggressive_takeover/` (no measured cue exists for "cut-off / unsafe overtake"); v4o gets only 9 of 160 such clips correct, mirroring the baseline.

**Why hints win uniquely on this category (73.1 > 65.0).** The hint payload is violation-targeted: for `aggressive_takeover` it asks *"Did the ego car cross a solid white or double yellow line to pass? Did the ego car cut off the other vehicle when merging back?"*; for `crossing_red_lights` *"Did the ego car's front bumper cross the stop line while the light was red?"*; for `crossing_stop` *"Did the ego car come to a complete halt (0 km/h) at the sign? Did the ego car yield to cross-traffic before proceeding?"*. These prompts directly furnish the missing violation vocabulary that all baselines and even v4o lack. The result on `aggressive_takeover` is dramatic: 5.6% → **85.6%** (+128 correct), because the hint converts forward-motion + visible-stop-sign into a yield-failure verdict that v4o never reaches. *(v4p, aggressive_takeover/agg_tk_002_Sunny.mp4)*: "consistent forward motion... The car proceeds through the intersection without a complete halt, which is not compliant with traffic rules that require stopping at a stop sign" — versus the v4o sibling that called the same clip a compliant highway transit. The trade-off is real: `crossing_red_lights` follow-rules drops 94.7% → 72.2% and `crossing_stop` drops 75.6% → 54.7% as v4p over-applies the "stop-sign yielding" template even where it does not apply (4 / 63 / 37 v4o-correct → v4p-wrong by subset). Net, the +128 aggressive-takeover wins outweigh the −63 / −37 regressions on the other two folders, producing the only category-level gain for hints in the benchmark. The hint-helped diff is also visible on the light-cue question (83.7% → 85.0%, +21 helped / 19 hurt), where the explicit "Did the front bumper cross the stop line while red?" cue overrides v4o's residual tendency to read pink-flow-plus-red-light as compliant entry under a hallucinated green. The implication mirrors the inverse finding in the Safety report: hints help wherever the failure is a *missing-vocabulary* problem (Traffic) and hurt wherever the failure is a *language-prior-overruling-tool-output* problem (Safety, Reality, Artifacts).
